Radiation Protection in Nuclear Power Plants

Radiation protection is a fundamental pillar in the safe operation of nuclear power plants. Its primary goal is to minimize radiation exposure for workers, the public, and the environment, following the ALARA principle (As Low As Reasonably Achievable).

On this platform, you will find key information about radiation protection principles, international regulations, radiation monitoring, and best practices used in the nuclear industry to ensure safety.
